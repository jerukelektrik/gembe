# Ruangguru Organic Leads & Conversion Initiative Design

Date: 2026-06-03
Scope: Organic Search, January-May 2026 baseline, Ruangguru mainsite and Blog RG

## Context

Ruangguru's Inbound team reported a decline in leads and conversion quality. WEB & SEO needs a 3-month initiative to increase organic leads and paid conversion while ensuring that lead quality improves, not just lead volume.

The initiative will focus on assets owned by the WEB & SEO team:

- Mainsite: https://ruangguru.com/
- Blog RG: https://ruangguru.com/blog

Lead definitions:

- Leads: users who fill a form, click a banner, or click WhatsApp.
- Conversion: users who purchase a product.
- Conversion categories: self purchase, inbound, and outbound.

## Baseline Diagnosis

Organic search already brings meaningful demand, but the conversion profile differs sharply between mainsite and Blog RG.

Mainsite organic search, January-May 2026:

- Sessions: 674,122
- Leads: 26,030
- Paid conversions: 1,762
- Revenue: Rp1,924,046,425
- Visit-to-lead CR: about 3.86%
- Leads-to-paid CR: about 6.77%

Blog RG organic search, January-May 2026:

- Sessions: 10,330,182
- Leads: 18,730
- Paid conversions: 513
- Revenue: Rp496,272,138
- Visit-to-lead CR: about 0.18%
- Leads-to-paid CR: about 2.74%

Interpretation:

- Mainsite has lower organic traffic than Blog RG, but stronger conversion density and a healthier leads-to-paid profile.
- Blog RG drives very large organic traffic, but much of the traffic is still weakly connected to qualified lead capture and paid conversion.
- The problem is not only organic traffic volume. The main opportunity is to turn organic traffic into a more relevant, higher-quality demand capture system.

Deck narrative:

> Organic search already brings meaningful demand, especially from Blog RG, but the conversion path is under-optimized. The initiative should shift organic from traffic-led acquisition into quality-led demand capture.

## Success Metrics

Primary KPIs:

- Organic leads increase.
- Organic paid conversion increases.
- Organic revenue increases.

Quality guardrail:

- Inbound leads-to-paid CR must increase versus the January-May 2026 baseline.

Supporting metrics:

- Visit-to-lead CR by landing page and page group.
- Leads-to-paid CR by landing page and page group.
- Revenue per lead.
- Lead split by channel: inbound, outbound, self purchase.
- CTA, form, banner, and WhatsApp click contribution by page or page group.

Because the team does not yet have lead quality labels such as qualified, unqualified, unreachable, or wrong segment, the initiative will use inbound leads-to-paid CR as the main quality proxy. Revenue per lead and paid conversion by landing page will be used as secondary quality signals.

## Strategic Approaches Considered

### 1. Quality-first conversion path optimization

Focus on pages and entry points that already show healthy conversion signals. Improve CTA placement, form flows, WhatsApp routing, banner relevance, and product recommendation.

Tradeoff: fastest path to impact, but depends on existing high-signal pages.

### 2. Blog intent segmentation

Treat Blog RG as multiple intent pools instead of one generic traffic source. Segment blog content into informational, exam or tryout, product-adjacent, and high-intent pages. Match CTA and offer by intent.

Tradeoff: requires content mapping and a more disciplined experimentation model, but directly addresses lead quality.

### 3. Organic high-intent expansion

Build or optimize SEO pages closer to purchase intent, such as product by grade, learning needs, exam prep, trial, promo, comparison, and product discovery pages.

Tradeoff: medium-term SEO impact, but healthier for sustainable growth.

Recommended approach:

- Combine approach 1 and 2 as the first 2-month push.
- Use approach 3 in month 3 to scale high-quality organic demand.

## Initiative Pillars

### 1. Organic Lead Quality Scorecard

Create a simple page-level scoring system for organic landing pages using available signals:

- Sessions
- Leads
- Paid conversions
- Leads-to-paid CR
- Revenue per lead
- Inbound contribution

Each page or page group should be classified into one of four actions:

- Optimize: strong intent or conversion signal, but conversion path can be improved.
- Scale: strong conversion profile and enough proof to expand content or internal linking.
- Nurture: useful traffic, but low purchase readiness; use soft capture or product education.
- Deprioritize: high traffic but weak conversion and low strategic relevance.

### 2. High-Intent Conversion Path Optimization

Optimize mainsite and blog pages that already generate paid conversion or healthy inbound conversion signals.

Candidate changes:

- Improve CTA placement and hierarchy.
- Refresh banner copy and offer relevance.
- Route WhatsApp and form leads to the right product or team.
- Add page-specific product recommendations.
- Reduce generic CTA usage on pages with clear intent.

Principle:

- Do not add more CTAs blindly. Match the conversion path to the user's intent.

### 3. Blog Intent Segmentation

Segment Blog RG content by user intent and assign CTA or offer patterns accordingly.

Suggested segments:

- Informational: general learning content, broad academic references, low purchase readiness.
- Exam or tryout: SNBT, TKA, school exams, simulation, tryout, and result discussion content.
- Product-adjacent: pages that mention Ruangguru products, learning programs, or learning problems.
- High-intent: pages where users are likely comparing solutions, looking for programs, or ready to take action.

Example CTA strategy:

- Informational pages: soft capture, newsletter, related learning guide, or product education.
- Exam or tryout pages: diagnostic test, tryout, exam prep product, or consultation for exam preparation.
- Product-adjacent pages: product recommendation, consultation, free trial, or promo.
- High-intent pages: direct consultation, WhatsApp, form, or self-purchase path.

### 4. Product-led SEO Expansion

Create or optimize SEO pages that are closer to purchase intent across all Ruangguru products.

Potential page themes:

- Product by school level or grade.
- Learning needs by student or parent problem.
- Exam preparation by test type.
- Trial, promo, and subscription discovery.
- Product comparison and recommendation.
- Product landing pages that answer common buyer questions.

This pillar supports medium-term growth after quick wins are validated.

### 5. Inbound Feedback Loop Lite

Start a lightweight lead quality feedback loop with the Inbound team.

Suggested fields:

- Good fit or poor fit.
- Product mismatch.
- Wrong segment.
- Unreachable.
- Low intent.
- Already purchased.
- Needs follow-up.

This does not need to be a full CRM project at the start. The goal is to give WEB & SEO a simple signal to improve page scoring, CTA selection, and lead routing.

## 3-Month Roadmap

### Month 1: Fix & Focus

Objective:

- Establish baseline and run fast fixes on high-signal organic pages.

Workstreams:

- Build organic funnel baseline by asset, page, lead channel, and conversion type.
- Create initial landing page scorecard.
- Identify top mainsite and blog pages by paid conversion, leads-to-paid CR, revenue per lead, and inbound contribution.
- Refresh CTA, banner, form, and WhatsApp routing on priority pages.
- Clean up tracking taxonomy for CTA, form, banner, WhatsApp, source page, and intent segment.

Expected output:

- Prioritized page list.
- First batch of conversion path improvements.
- Baseline dashboard or reporting view for weekly tracking.

### Month 2: Segment & Experiment

Objective:

- Match conversion path and offer by page intent.

Workstreams:

- Classify blog and mainsite organic pages into intent groups.
- Run CTA and offer experiments by segment.
- Reduce aggressive inbound capture on low-intent informational pages.
- Route exam or tryout pages to diagnostic, tryout, or exam prep offers.
- Route product-adjacent pages to product recommendation or consultation.
- Strengthen self-purchase path for high-intent pages.

Expected output:

- Intent-based CTA and banner system.
- Experiment readout by page group.
- Clear view of which segments increase leads without hurting inbound leads-to-paid CR.

### Month 3: Scale & Product-led Growth

Objective:

- Scale validated patterns and expand high-quality organic demand.

Workstreams:

- Scale winning CTA, banner, routing, and self-purchase patterns.
- Create or optimize product-led SEO pages for all products.
- Improve internal linking from high-traffic blog pages to relevant product-led pages.
- Use Inbound feedback to refine lead quality scoring.
- Build an organic lead quality playbook for repeatable WEB & SEO operations.

Expected output:

- Scaled conversion improvements.
- Product-led SEO backlog.
- Organic lead quality playbook.

## Candidate Experiments

1. Organic Landing Page Scorecard

- Rank mainsite and Blog RG pages by leads, paid conversion, inbound quality proxy, revenue per lead, and traffic.
- Assign each page to optimize, scale, nurture, or deprioritize.

2. High-Intent CTA and WhatsApp Routing Refresh

- Improve CTA, banner, and routing on pages already generating paid conversion.
- Replace generic CTAs with page-specific actions.

3. Blog Intent-Based Banner System

- Create banner and CTA variants for exam or tryout, materi sekolah, product-related, promo, and general informational pages.
- Avoid using the same acquisition CTA across all blog traffic.

4. Organic Product Recommendation Entry Point

- Add product recommendation entry points on pages where the audience has a clear need but may not know which product fits.
- Use this as pre-qualification before sending users to Inbound.

5. High-Intent SEO Landing Pages

- Build or optimize pages for product by grade, exam prep, trial, promo, comparison, and parent or student needs.

6. Inbound Feedback Loop Lite

- Collect lightweight reasons why leads are low quality.
- Feed this back into scoring, CTA decisions, and page prioritization.

7. Self-Purchase Path Lift

- For high-intent pages, strengthen direct purchase paths so ready buyers are not forced into consultation.

## Operating Model

Weekly rhythm:

- Review top organic landing pages by traffic, leads, paid conversion, leads-to-paid CR, revenue per lead, and inbound contribution.
- Select pages for optimize, scale, nurture, or deprioritize.
- Review experiment performance by intent segment.
- Capture simple Inbound feedback on lead quality.
- Decide whether to scale, revise, or stop each experiment.

Decision rules:

- A change is successful if it increases leads or paid conversion while maintaining or improving inbound leads-to-paid CR.
- A lead growth tactic should not be scaled if it increases low-intent inbound workload and lowers inbound leads-to-paid CR.
- High-traffic informational pages can remain valuable as traffic feeders, but they should not automatically become direct inbound lead capture pages.
- High-intent pages should support both consultation and self-purchase paths.

## Risks And Controls

Risk: Blog traffic volume creates many low-intent leads.

Control:

- Segment blog intent and avoid aggressive direct inbound CTA on broad informational pages.

Risk: More CTAs create more leads but lower lead quality.

Control:

- Use inbound leads-to-paid CR as the scaling guardrail.

Risk: Inbound feedback is unavailable or inconsistent.

Control:

- Start with a lightweight feedback loop and use conversion proxy metrics until quality labels become reliable.

Risk: Product-led SEO pages take time to rank.

Control:

- Pair medium-term SEO expansion with month 1 and month 2 conversion quick wins.
