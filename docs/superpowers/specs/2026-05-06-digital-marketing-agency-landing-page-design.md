# Digital Marketing Agency Landing Page Design

Date: 2026-05-06

## Goal

Create a landing page for a digital marketing agency that offers web development, local SEO, and monthly maintenance for local SMEs and professional service businesses.

The first version should help the business start selling. It should position the agency as a practical, credible, and affordable website partner, with WhatsApp as the primary conversion path.

## Target Audience

The landing page is for:

- Local SMEs that need a professional website.
- Professional service businesses such as clinics, consultants, contractors, salons, local shops, and service providers.

The page should avoid heavy technical language. It should speak to business owners who care about trust, clear packages, simple process, and getting contacted by potential customers.

## Positioning

Primary positioning:

> Website profesional untuk bisnis lokal yang ingin terlihat lebih terpercaya.

Supporting message:

> Kami bantu UMKM dan jasa profesional punya website rapi, mudah dipahami, dan siap dihubungi lewat WhatsApp.

The offer should not look like a generic digital agency. It should feel like a focused partner for credible business websites, with local SEO and maintenance as natural follow-up services.

## Conversion Flow

The main conversion path is:

1. Visitor understands the trust-focused value proposition in the hero.
2. Visitor sees the common business problems the agency solves.
3. Visitor reviews the three core services.
4. Visitor sees example website categories or portfolio placeholders.
5. Visitor understands the agency framework.
6. Visitor reviews trust-building FAQ content.
7. Visitor contacts the agency through WhatsApp.

Primary CTA: WhatsApp consultation.

Secondary CTA: view examples or portfolio section.

## Page Structure

### Hero

Purpose: communicate the main promise quickly and direct users to WhatsApp.

Content:

- Headline about professional websites for trusted local businesses.
- Short supporting copy about clear, credible, WhatsApp-ready websites.
- Primary CTA to WhatsApp.
- Secondary CTA to examples or portfolio.

### Problem Section

Purpose: make the visitor feel understood before showing services.

Problems to cover:

- The business already exists, but has no professional website.
- Information is scattered across social media or chat.
- The current online presence does not build enough trust.
- Potential customers struggle to understand services, location, or how to contact the business.

### Services

Purpose: present three clear services without overwhelming the visitor.

Services:

1. Website Profesional
   - Company profile, landing page, service pages, and WhatsApp-ready CTAs.
2. SEO Lokal
   - Basic local SEO, service/location page structure, and Google visibility foundations.
3. Maintenance Bulanan
   - Content updates, small improvements, security checks, and ongoing website care.

Pricing does not need to be shown in the first version. If pricing is added later, use clear package language such as "mulai dari" or "konsultasi paket".

### Examples Or Portfolio

Purpose: build trust even before there are many case studies.

If real portfolio items are not available, show example website categories:

- Website Klinik
- Website Konsultan
- Website Kontraktor
- Website UMKM Lokal

These can later be replaced with real case studies.

### Framework

Purpose: make the agency feel methodical and more valuable than a basic website vendor.

Framework name:

**The Local Web Launch Framework**

Steps:

1. Discover
   - Understand the business, main services, target customers, service area, and best CTA.
2. Structure
   - Organize pages, copy, navigation, services, and visitor flow.
3. Build
   - Design and develop a responsive, fast, professional website.
4. Launch & Tracking
   - Connect domain and hosting, add WhatsApp CTA, apply basic SEO, install Google Tag Manager, Google Analytics, Google Search Console, and relevant Google tracking or pixel setup.
5. Care
   - Provide maintenance, content updates, small fixes, and optional local SEO optimization after launch.

This section should replace a generic "process" section or make the process section feel branded.

### Trust And FAQ

Purpose: reduce purchase anxiety.

FAQ topics:

- How long does a website take?
- Is revision included?
- Can the agency help with domain and hosting?
- Can the website content be updated later?
- Is SEO included?
- Does SEO guarantee ranking?
- Can the business start with website only and add SEO or maintenance later?

Answers should be direct, simple, and honest.

### Final CTA

Purpose: give one clear next step.

CTA message:

> Ceritakan bisnis kamu, kami bantu arahkan paket yang paling masuk akal.

Action: WhatsApp consultation link.

## Visual Direction

The page should feel modern, trustworthy, and practical.

Recommended direction:

- Light background using white or off-white.
- Dark, readable text.
- One professional accent color such as navy or teal.
- WhatsApp green used only for WhatsApp-specific CTAs.
- Clean typography with strong headings and comfortable body text.
- Small-radius cards and restrained shadows.
- Mobile-first layout.
- Clear spacing and fast scanning.

Avoid:

- Overly playful visuals.
- Generic "digital agency" gradients.
- Too many decorative effects.
- Technical SEO jargon in primary copy.
- Fake-looking portfolio or exaggerated claims.

## UX Requirements

- Primary CTA should be available in the hero and final CTA.
- On mobile, a subtle sticky WhatsApp CTA can appear after scrolling.
- Touch targets should be at least 44px.
- Text must remain readable on small screens.
- The secondary CTA should scroll to examples or portfolio.
- FAQs should be easy to scan.
- Each section should have one clear purpose.

## Technical Scope

The repository is currently a static HTML/CSS/JS project. The landing page can be implemented as a static page without a backend.

Technical requirements:

- Use static HTML, CSS, and JavaScript consistent with the existing project.
- Use placeholder WhatsApp `wa.me` link until the real phone number is provided.
- Keep portfolio/example content static in the first version.
- Do not add a form in the first version because WhatsApp is the chosen primary sales path.
- Ensure basic responsive behavior for mobile and desktop.

## Testing And Verification

Verify:

- Desktop layout.
- Mobile layout.
- Hero CTA opens WhatsApp.
- Final CTA opens WhatsApp.
- Secondary CTA scrolls to examples or portfolio.
- FAQ interactions work if accordions are used.
- No horizontal scrolling on mobile.
- Page remains readable and fast with no heavy assets.

## Out Of Scope

- Backend lead capture form.
- Payment or deposit flow.
- Calendar booking integration.
- Full brand identity system.
- Real case study writing unless provided later.
- Advertising campaign setup.
- Social media management pages.
